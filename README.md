# ReadytogoMars
The story it tells: Earth → Launch → Deep Space → Seven Minutes of Terror → Mars Surface → Home
<br>

"So I was given this challenge — build an interactive storytelling website. And I chose the Journey to Mars theme.
But I didn't want to just make a website with some animations. I wanted someone to actually feel like they're on the mission.
So I started with Earth. Because every journey needs a beginning. You see the planet, the atmosphere, the satellite orbiting — and then you scroll, and you leave it behind.
Then comes the launch. There's a fully animated rocket with a real exhaust particle system, live telemetry counters ticking in real time, and a countdown that actually fires when you scroll in.
The deep space section uses a pinned horizontal scroll — so instead of just scrolling down, you travel sideways through time. Day 1. Day 94. Day 180. Each card is a moment from the journey.
Then the landing — a parachute deploys, retro rockets fire, and the lander touches down on Mars.
And finally, a canvas-rendered rover actually drives across the Martian surface in real time.
The whole thing is tied together with GSAP ScrollTrigger, a custom animated cursor, mouse parallax on the planets, and a twinkling starfield.
Seven sections. One continuous story. Built in pure HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
This is Journey to Mars — Mission 2047."
